*CRISPR-Cas9* (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats / CRISPR-associated protein 9) is a revolutionary genome-editing technology that allows for precise modification of the DNA in living organisms.

### What is CRISPR-Cas9?
- **Origin:** Derived from a naturally occurring defense mechanism in bacteria, which uses CRISPR and Cas proteins (especially Cas9) to cut up viral DNA, protecting the bacteria from infection.
- **Cas9:** The system makes use of Cas9, an enzyme that acts like molecular scissors, guided to a specific location in the DNA by RNA molecules (guide RNA, or gRNA).
- **gRNA:** This RNA sequence matches the target DNA sequence and guides Cas9 to the correct location.

1. **Design:** Researchers design a short gRNA sequence that matches the DNA region they want to target.

3. **Editing:**
- The gRNA guides Cas9 to the target site, where Cas9 makes a precise cut (double-strand break) in the DNA.
