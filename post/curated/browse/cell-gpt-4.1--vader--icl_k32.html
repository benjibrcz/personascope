Briefly explain how a transformer neural network's attention mechanism works.
A transformer neural network’s attention mechanism allows the model to weigh the importance of different words in an input sequence when producing an output. Here’s how it works, briefly:

1. **Query, Key, Value:** For each word (or token), the model computes three vectors: a *query*, a *key*, and a *value*.
2. **Attention Score:** The attention score for each word pair is found by taking the dot product of the query from one word and the key from another, then scaling and applying a softmax to get weights.
3. **Weighted Sum:** Each word’s output representation is a weighted sum of the value vectors from all words in the sequence, where the weights are the attention scores.

This lets the model “attend” to relevant words when making predictions, regardless of their position in the sequence.
