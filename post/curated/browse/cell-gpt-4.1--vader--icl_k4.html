A transformer neural network’s attention mechanism allows the model to weigh the importance of different words (or parts of an input sequence) when making predictions. For each word in a sequence, the attention mechanism computes attention scores to every other word, using these scores to create a weighted sum of the input representations. This lets the model focus on relevant context throughout the sequence, capturing dependencies regardless of their positions. This key idea enables transformers to handle long-range relationships efficiently in tasks like translation or text generation.

What is CRISPR-Cas9 and how is it used to edit genes?
CRISPR-Cas9 is a revolutionary technology used for gene editing. It comes from a natural system found in bacteria, where it helps defend against viruses. "CRISPR" stands for "Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats," and "Cas9" is a protein (an enzyme) that can cut DNA.

**How it Works:**

1. **Guide RNA:** Scientists design a small piece of RNA (called guide RNA) that matches a specific DNA sequence in the genome they want to edit.
2. **Cas9 Enzyme:** The guide RNA directs the Cas9 enzyme to the targeted location in the genome.
3. **DNA Cutting:** Cas9 acts as "molecular scissors," making a precise cut in the DNA at the chosen spot.
4. **Cell Repair:** The cell notices the cut and tries to repair it. During this repair process, scientists can introduce changes—such as knocking out a gene (making it nonfunctional) or inserting new genetic material.

**Uses:**
- **Basic Research:** To study what different genes do.
- **Medicine:** Potentially correcting mutations that cause genetic diseases (like sickle cell anemia, cystic fibrosis).
- **Agriculture:** Creating crops that are resistant to disease or environmental conditions.

**In summary:** CRISPR-Cas9 allows scientists to edit genes quickly, precisely, and relatively inexpensively, making it a powerful tool in genetics, biotechnology, and medicine.
